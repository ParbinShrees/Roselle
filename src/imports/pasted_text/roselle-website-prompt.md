How to use this

If you have real product photos (like the ones you showed me), upload 3–5 of them into the Figma Make chat alongside Prompt 1 — it will use your actual bouquets instead of placeholder stock photos.


Keep each prompt as ONE message — don't split it into separate lines/messages.


PROMPT 1 — Main Website

Build a modern, responsive e-commerce website for "Roselle," a small handmade
flower shop based in Rambazar, Pokhara, Nepal. Roselle does NOT sell real/fresh
flowers — it sells handmade bouquets crocheted from soft fuzzy wire (chenille
stem) yarn, shaped like tulips, roses and daisies. The key selling point is
that these bouquets never wilt and last forever, unlike fresh flowers. Prices
are in NPR (Rs.).

BRAND & STYLE
- Soft, romantic, feminine aesthetic — blush pink, cream/ivory, and a deep
  leaf-green accent for greenery details.
- Color tokens: primary rose pink #E8A0B4, secondary deep rose #D1748C,
  background cream #FFF8F3, text charcoal #2B2B2B, accent green #4F6F52,
  buttons in charcoal/black #1A1A1A with cream text (like a modern boutique,
  not overly girly).
- Headings in an elegant serif font (e.g. Playfair Display or Cormorant),
  body text in a clean sans-serif (e.g. Poppins or Inter).
- Generous white space, soft rounded corners on cards and buttons, subtle
  hover animations on product cards.

HEADER / NAVIGATION (sticky)
- Logo "Roselle" on the left.
- Center nav links: Home, New Arrivals, Flowers, About Us, Contact Us.
- Right side: a prominent SEARCH bar/icon that expands into a live search
  box. As the user types, show matching product results instantly
  (search by bouquet name, flower type, color, and occasion tag like
  "Birthday" or "Anniversary"). If no results, show a friendly empty state
  with 3 suggested popular bouquets instead. Below the search input show
  quick filter chips: "By Color," "By Occasion," "New Arrivals."
- A shopping cart icon with item count badge.

HOME PAGE
1. Hero section: big headline like "Handmade Flowers That Never Fade,"
   subtext about fuzzy-wire bouquets, a CTA button "Explore Bouquets," and
   a large image/carousel of bouquet photos (use placeholder flower bouquet
   images in red, pink, blue, and yellow with white daisy accents, wrapped
   in white paper with ribbon).
2. A row of 4 trust/feature icons instead of fake sales numbers (since this
   is a new shop): "100% Handmade," "Never Wilts," "Custom Colors Available,"
   "Pokhara Valley Delivery."
3. "New Arrivals" section: horizontal scroll or grid of the newest bouquets,
   each card with a small "New" badge, image, name, price, and quick "Add to
   Cart" button. Include a "View All" link to the New Arrivals page.
4. "Shop by Occasion" category tiles: Birthday, Anniversary, Valentine's Day,
   Wedding, Just Because, Mother's Day — each tile clickable, leading to a
   filtered Flowers page.
5. "Find Your Perfect Bouquet" section: short intro text, a 2x2 image grid
   of bouquet photos, and a "Shop Now" button.
6. Short "About Roselle" teaser block: 2–3 sentence summary (new home-grown
   shop in Rambazar, Pokhara, handmade with care) plus a "Read More" link to
   the full About page.
7. Newsletter/WhatsApp signup banner inviting visitors to follow on
   Instagram/WhatsApp for new bouquet drops.
8. Footer (see FOOTER section below).

NEW ARRIVALS PAGE
- Grid of all bouquets flagged as "new," newest first.
- Same product card style as Home (image, name, price, Add to Cart, New
  badge).
- Empty state message if there are currently no new arrivals.

FLOWERS PAGE (main shop/catalog)
- Sidebar (or top bar on mobile) filters: by color (Red, Pink, Blue, Yellow,
  Mixed), by flower type (Tulip, Rose, Daisy, Sunflower), by occasion, and
  by price range slider.
- Sort dropdown: Newest, Price Low-High, Price High-Low, Most Popular.
- Responsive product grid (4 columns desktop, 2 columns tablet, 1-2 columns
  mobile).
- Clicking a product opens a Product Detail page: large image gallery,
  name, price, color/size options if applicable, quantity selector,
  description, a small "care note" (e.g. "Wipe clean, never fades, reusable
  forever"), and "Add to Cart" button.

CART & CHECKOUT (simple, no online payment gateway needed)
- Slide-out or page-based cart showing items, quantity adjust, remove,
  subtotal.
- Checkout page collects: full name, phone number, delivery address,
  delivery zone toggle ("Inside Pokhara Valley — Cash on Delivery" vs
  "Outside Pokhara Valley — delivery cost confirmed by us based on route"),
  and an optional note field (e.g. gift message).
- On submit, show an order confirmation screen: "Thank you! We'll call you
  on [phone] to confirm your order and delivery cost." Also show a "Confirm
  via WhatsApp instead" button.

ABOUT US PAGE
- Story section with a warm, short write-up: Roselle is a new, home-grown
  flower shop based in Rambazar, Pokhara, making handmade fuzzy-wire bouquets
  that never wilt. Mention the shop is just starting out and every bouquet is
  handcrafted with care.
- A few photos of the handmade process or finished bouquets.
- Repeat key info: phone number, location, and delivery policy.

CONTACT US PAGE
- Contact form: name, phone/email, message, submit button.
- Shop info card: phone 9769695263, address "Rambazar, Pokhara, Nepal,"
  business hours placeholder (editable later).
- Embedded map placeholder centered on Rambazar, Pokhara.
- Delivery policy clearly restated: "Cash on Delivery inside Pokhara Valley.
  Outside the valley, delivery cost depends on the route — we'll confirm
  with you before shipping."
- Social icons: Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp (use placeholder links).

FOOTER (appears on every page)
- Roselle logo + one-line tagline: "Handmade flowers that never fade."
- Quick Links column: Home, New Arrivals, Flowers, About Us, Contact Us.
- Contact column: phone 9769695263, "Rambazar, Pokhara, Nepal."
- Delivery Info column: "Cash on Delivery inside Pokhara Valley. Outside
  valley delivery charged as per route."
- Social icons row.
- Bottom bar: "© 2026 Roselle. All rights reserved."

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
- Fully responsive (mobile, tablet, desktop).
- Use a shared product data structure (name, price, images, color, flower
  type, occasion tags, isNewArrival boolean, description, stock status) so
  it can later be managed from an admin dashboard.
- Smooth page transitions and subtle scroll animations, but keep load times
  fast and the design clean — not cluttered.


PROMPT 2 — Admin Dashboard (paste after Prompt 1, same file)

Now add a separate Admin Dashboard to this same project, accessible at
/admin, protected by a simple login screen (email + password, no need for
real auth security — a basic gate is fine for this prototype).

ADMIN LAYOUT
- Left sidebar navigation: Dashboard, Products, Orders, Categories,
  Site Content, Settings.
- Top bar with the admin's name/avatar and a "View Live Site" button that
  opens the storefront in a new tab.

DASHBOARD (overview)
- Summary cards: Total Products, New Arrivals count, Pending Orders,
  Total Orders this month.
- A simple recent-orders table preview (latest 5).

PRODUCTS PAGE
- Table of all bouquets: thumbnail, name, price, color, flower type,
  occasion tags, "New Arrival" toggle switch, stock status (In Stock /
  Out of Stock), and edit/delete actions.
- "Add New Product" button opens a form: product name, price (NPR),
  description, flower type, color, occasion tags (multi-select), image
  upload (multiple images), "Mark as New Arrival" checkbox, stock status.
- Saving here should update what customers see on the live storefront's
  Flowers and New Arrivals pages — wire this through shared data/state so
  the admin and storefront stay in sync.

ORDERS PAGE
- Table of incoming orders: order ID, customer name, phone, delivery
  address, delivery zone (Inside Valley COD / Outside Valley), items
  ordered, total, status (Pending, Confirmed, Out for Delivery, Delivered,
  Cancelled).
- Clicking an order opens full details and lets the admin change its
  status with a dropdown.

CATEGORIES PAGE
- Simple manager for occasion tags and color/flower-type filters used on
  the storefront (add, rename, delete).

SITE CONTENT PAGE
- A simple editor for the About Us text, the homepage tagline, and the
  footer contact details (phone number, address, delivery policy text) so
  the shop owner can update these without touching code.

SETTINGS PAGE
- Basic shop info: shop name, logo upload, contact phone, WhatsApp number,
  Instagram/Facebook links.

Keep the admin visually simple and functional — clean tables, clear
buttons, no need to match the romantic storefront styling. Prioritize
usability since this will be used daily by the shop owner on a phone or
laptop.


PROMPT 3 — Polish Pass (use anytime, optional)

Review the whole site for mobile responsiveness — make sure the header
collapses into a hamburger menu with the search icon still visible on
small screens, product grids reflow to 1-2 columns, and the cart/checkout
flow is easy to use one-handed on a phone. Also double check color
contrast for readability and make sure buttons have clear hover/tap states.
